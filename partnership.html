Whelen
Modified Tours

  • Modified racing is the oldest division in NASCAR, dating back to 1948
  • The Modified Tour is based primarily in the Northeast, with a concentration in the New England region
  • The Southern Modified Tour was established in 2007 and is based primarily in the Southeast region of the United States
  • Both of the tours feature open-wheeled race cars without fenders
  • Modified race cars feature 625 horsepower (NASCAR-approved SPEC engine is optional) and weigh 2,645 pounds (including driver) while running on Hoosier tires
  • TV coverage on Fox Sports 1

About the NASCAR
Hall of Fame

Conveniently located in Uptown Charlotte, N.C., the 150,000-square-foot NASCAR Hall of Fame is an interactive, entertainment attraction honoring the history and heritage of NASCAR. The high-tech venue, designed to educate and entertain race fans and non-fans alike, opened May 11, 2010, and includes artifacts, hands-on exhibits, a 278-person state-of-the-art theater, Hall of Honor, NASCAR Hall of Fame Gear Shop and NASCAR Media Group-operated broadcast studio.

The venue is open seven days a week and has an attached parking garage on Brevard Street. The five-acre site also includes a privately developed 19-story office tower and 102,000-square-foot expansion to the Charlotte Convention Center, highlighted by a 40,000 square-foot ballroom. The NHOF is owned by the City of Charlotte, licensed by NASCAR and operated by the Charlotte Regional Visitors Authority.
